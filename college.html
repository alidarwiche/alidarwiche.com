But wait, there's more!

As an Honors Creative Writing student, I was fortunate enough to complete my own thesis containing a collection of my poetry. This collection is titled "Find Me" and is an exploration of the reader/writer relationship. This exploration follows and is meant to address my own anxiety with the concept of a "reader". Up until this collection, there was no reader involved in my work; only myself. To me, poetry was this safe space where I could experiment with word and sound to discover new ways of portraying emotion. To be honest, it still is. But, for this collection, my experimental thoughts needed to make sense to a reader. In that way I had to satisfy the reader, and that scares me. What is this relationship between the reader and the writer? What is expected of each of us? What is fair?

I intent to continue growing this collection, and will be avoiding posting it online until it is complete. If you would like to read my collection, send me a private message. Twitter or LinkedIn will work if you do not have my contact info. I would love to hear your thoughts on my work, thus far!
